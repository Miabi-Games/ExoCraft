# ExoCraft

**ExoCraft** is a voxel/block-based automation survival game prototype built with Godot. Its current source code is publicly available under the MIT License.

The project is inspired by games such as *FortressCraft Evolved* and Minecraft-style block worlds, but with a stronger focus on embodied survival, settlement, and home-making. The player is not only building machines and expanding a base; they are creating a place where people can live, recover, work, rest, and eventually want more than mere survival.

> The factory is not only for production. The factory is how the player makes a home.

## Project Status

ExoCraft is in early development.

At this stage, the project is primarily a prototype and portfolio piece intended to explore:

* voxel/block-based world interaction
* survival systems such as hunger, sleep, shelter, and livable conditions
* wants, comfort, and quality-of-life systems
* automation and resource processing
* Godot game architecture
* clean, maintainable gameplay code

Expect rapid changes while the core design and technical foundation are being established.

## Core Concept

ExoCraft combines survival pressure, automation progression, and the gradual creation of a livable home.

Early in the game, the player should feel vulnerable: gathering resources by hand, finding or producing food, sleeping in rough shelters, and trying to stay alive in an unfamiliar environment.

As the game progresses, survival itself becomes part of the automation challenge. Food production, safe sleeping areas, environmental control, storage, resource processing, and base infrastructure should gradually become systems the player can build, improve, and automate.

But ExoCraft is not only about meeting minimum survival needs. A base should grow from a desperate shelter into a home: a place that can provide safety, comfort, routine, beauty, privacy, community, and purpose. The player’s creations should not only keep people alive; they should make life better.

## Design Goals

ExoCraft is being designed around a few guiding ideas:

* **Survival as infrastructure**
  Food, sleep, shelter, and safety are not just meters. They are systems the player can solve through building and automation.

* **Home as progression**
  The player is not simply expanding a factory. They are turning an unfamiliar place into somewhere people can live, belong, and want to improve.

* **Wants beyond needs**
  Once basic survival is secure, people should begin to care about comfort, dignity, beauty, social life, and other quality-of-life concerns.

* **A physical voxel world**
  The world is made of blocks that can be mined, placed, processed, and shaped into functional spaces.

* **Automation with purpose**
  Machines are not only for producing more machines. They help the player create and sustain a livable home in a hostile or unfamiliar environment.

* **Small scope, strong foundation**
  ExoCraft is intended to be smaller and more achievable than larger engineering-sandbox ideas, while still demonstrating solid game-system design.

* **No modding support planned**
  The project is open source, but formal modding support is outside the intended scope.
